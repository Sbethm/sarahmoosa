...you were a Greek Philosophy student?

Who would you study under? The phrophetic Pythagoras, the great Socrates, or the brilliant Aristotle. Learn more about these great philosophers and take the quiz to see where you would fall in history.

Pythagoras

Pythagoras was the first person recorded to call himself a philosopher, "a lover of wisdom". He lived from 570-495 B.C., and his teachings influenced future philosophers such as Plato and Aristotle. Supposedly, genius mathematician and a little bit of a mystic.

Socrates

Socrates is considered the first moral philosopher and had lived from 470-399 B.C. During his time, people thought that beauty was a sign of good virtue. As one of the ugliest men, he stood to question this idea and what good virtue was. He questioned everything and everyone leading the way for future, logical thinkers.

Aristotle

Aristotle was a man of many interests and is believed to be the father of our modern scientific method. He lived from 384-322 B.C. He studied everything from biology to astrology. Many of the terms he used in his studies are still used in many scientific fields today.
