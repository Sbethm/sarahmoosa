Four Seasons of Yuzawa

On the island country of Japan, a small village is nestled among the wild, northern mountains.

Winter

雪

Snow. The one word that best describes winter in Yuzawa. Feet and feet of snow fall to blanket the city for a long, dark winter, but with warm locals and hot sake, you just might survive.

スキー

Snow plus mountains equals skiing! There are plenty of places to ski for any skill level, from gentle slopes for the bunnies to steep drops for the dare devils.

きりたんぽ

Kiritanpo is a delicious, hot broth full of winter vegetables, meat, and the all important mashed rice sticks! A perfect soup to warm anyone up throughout a bleak winter trapped indoor.

Spring

花

Spring comes to Yuzawa in a burst of color! Flowers cover the landscape as the days lengthen and people begin to creep out of the winter hibernation.

花見

One of the first flowers to greet Yuzawa are the famous cherry blossoms. The best way to enjoy these delicate, pink blossoms is to view them while picnicking and drinking local sake. Everything pairs well with sake.

桜桃

At the edge of Yuzawa are forests of cherry farms, but don't be fooled, these cherries don't come from the cherry blossoms. You can buy these cherries at any local store or you can sit under a tree and eat until you burst!

Summer

青葉

The summer in Yuzawa is short and hot. Throughout the city, rice fields are planted among the homes, the clean water and sweet rice that grows here makes for some of the best sake in Japan.

七夕絵

Tanabata is a famous festival throughout Japan, but in Yuzawa, people draw beautiful art on paper lanterns and line the streets with them. At night, the lanterns glow while people walk around admiring the artwork and enjoying different festival events.

焼肉

The perfect way to enjoy the cool summer nights is to gather your friends together and have a traditional Japanese BBQ. On small skewers, you put meats and local vegetables that you cook over a low fire. It takes time, but with good friends and good drink, the nights always end too quickly.

Autumn

紅葉

As summer draws to an end and autumn approaches, the trees begin　to turn shades of yellow, orange, and red setting forest afire with their vibrant colors.

山登り

As winter approaches, the people of Yuzawa board up windows and build shelters for their delicate trees. Known for its tremendous amount of snowfall, Yuzawa is blanketed by deep drifts of soft, white snow every winter. Windows
